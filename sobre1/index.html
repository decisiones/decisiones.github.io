About Sponsors

Veracel Celulose is a Brazilian company located in southern Bahia, dedicated to the sustainable production of bleached eucalyptus pulp. Formed as a joint venture between the Swedish-Finnish Stora Enso and the Brazilian Suzano Papel e Celulose, Veracel adopts advanced forest management and environmental conservation practices, balancing industrial production with socio-environmental responsibility. The company operates one of the largest pulp mills in Latin America, generating employment and economic development in the region.
A notable initiative by Veracel is the maintenance of conservation areas, such as the Private Reserve of the Natural Heritage (RPPN) Esta o Veracel, located in Porto Seguro, Bahia. Established in 1998, this RPPN protects approximately 6,000 hectares of Atlantic Forest, one of the most biodiverse and threatened biomes in Brazil. The site is recognized by UNESCO as part of the Atlantic Forest Biosphere Reserve and plays a crucial role in the conservation of endangered species, such as the golden-headed lion tamarin. Additionally, the reserve promotes scientific research, environmental education, and sustainable tourism, reinforcing Veracel's commitment to ecosystem preservation and biodiversity enhancement.

Visiting Location:

Private Reserve of the Natural Heritage (RPPN) - Esta o Veracel

Located in the municipalities of Porto Seguro and Santa Cruz Cabr lia, in southern Bahia, Esta o Veracel is an area of more than 6,000 hectares of preserved Atlantic Forest, recognized by UNESCO as a Natural World Heritage Site.

The reserve plays a fundamental role in protecting endangered species and provides essential ecosystem services, such as the preservation of 115 springs that benefit the region and contribute to the local and global climatic balance.

By connecting forest fragments, Esta o Veracel promotes biodiversity by enabling genetic exchange between species, fostering the ecological viability of the region.

The area is also an important center for scientific research and environmental education, providing a protected and monitored environment where activities are carried out with children, youth, and researchers from local communities and other regions.

With infrastructure that integrates nature and learning, Esta o Veracel is a reference in Atlantic Forest conservation and an unparalleled opportunity to experience environmental preservation initiatives. Explore photos, videos, and projects that highlight the relevance of this natural heritage.

Symbiosis is a Brazilian company located in Porto Seguro, Bahia, known for its commitment to sustainable development and the integration of innovative investment practices with respect for the environment. Operating in projects that combine economic impact and environmental conservation, Symbiosis focuses on promoting the responsible use of natural resources, valuing the biodiversity and environmental heritage of the region.
One of Symbiosis' notable initiatives is its involvement in conservation areas, such as the protection of environmental reserves in Porto Seguro, a territory with rich biodiversity and historical relevance. The company contributes to projects that preserve local ecosystems, fostering partnerships with communities, environmental education, and innovative solutions for sustainable development. These actions reflect its commitment to balancing economic progress and environmental conservation, reinforcing the importance of Porto Seguro as one of Brazil's main biodiversity hotspots.
Additionally, Symbiosis adopts a responsible operation, prioritizing tree planting over selective logging, with the aim of achieving a sustainable future. The company holds the FSC (Forest Stewardship Council) certification, which ensures that its wood comes from responsibly managed forests, following strict environmental, social, and economic criteria. Symbiosis is also part of Apple's Restore Fund, a fund created in 2021 with the goal of expanding natural solutions to combat climate change. This strategic partnership between Apple, Goldman Sachs, and Conservation International invests in projects that remove carbon from the atmosphere and promote long-term environmental and social benefits.
Based in the Atlantic Forest region, Symbiosis recognizes the immense reforestation potential of this biome, which has suffered significant habitat and biodiversity loss over the past 500 years. The company's mission is to cultivate this potential by combining wood production with carbon capture. Founded by Bruno Mariani, Symbiosis seeks to demonstrate that it is possible to reforest Brazil with native species, recovering soil and water, producing high-quality wood, and preventing the deforestation of natural forests.
